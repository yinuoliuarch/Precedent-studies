## Installation Aesthetics

SEEK appeared as a transparent, table-like environment: a shallow Plexiglas enclosure filled with a dense field of small metal-faced cubes, held beneath a rigid rectangular framework. The clear walls exposed the gerbils and the changing block landscape from every side, while the open mechanical structure above revealed the carriage, cables, and robotic arm. Nothing was concealed behind a finished architectural surface. The installation presented its habitat, machinery, and control system as one visible assembly.

Its appearance was shaped by a strong contrast between order and disruption. The repeated cubes formed a pale, modular grid associated with measurement and computational control, while the gerbils produced irregular paths, collapses, and gaps within it. Above them, the dark linear frame imposed a larger Cartesian geometry. The suspended arm moved vertically between these two visual registers, connecting the abstract order of the machine to the unstable terrain below.

At gallery scale, SEEK read simultaneously as a model, a laboratory apparatus, and a live spectacle. Viewers looked through the enclosure as if observing an experiment, yet the continuous motion prevented the work from becoming a static display. Its aesthetic depended on exposed process: the installation's appearance was continually remade by the tension between transparent containment, repetitive geometry, mechanical movement, and animal activity.
