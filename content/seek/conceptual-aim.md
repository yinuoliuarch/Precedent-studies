## Conceptual Aim

SEEK tested a central question of the Architecture Machine Group: can an environment become intelligent enough to understand and respond to its users? In the project, the “users” were not human designers but gerbils, whose behavior was spontaneous, embodied, and difficult to predict. This substitution made the experiment more radical. It asked whether architecture could become responsive not only to explicit commands, but also to indirect patterns of behavior.
