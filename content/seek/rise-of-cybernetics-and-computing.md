## Rise of Cybernetics and Computing

The development of SEEK coincided with the emergence of cybernetics and early computing technologies. Cybernetics understood machines, organisms, and environments through feedback: a system senses a change, compares it with an internal model, and acts in response. This shifted computation from a tool used only for calculation toward an active participant in a changing physical environment.

SEEK translated this theory into an architectural installation. Its computer maintained a model of the block landscape, while the robotic arm attempted to reconcile that model with the disturbances produced by the gerbils. The project therefore made the limits of early machine intelligence visible as well as its ambitions. Computation could repeatedly sense and intervene, but it could not fully understand or control the unpredictable life unfolding inside the enclosure.
