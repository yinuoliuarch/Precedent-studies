## Bibliography

The bibliography is divided into sources by Negroponte or the Architecture Machine Group, and sources written by others that critique, document, or contextualize SEEK.

### What the Author Has Written

1. **Negroponte, Nicholas. “Seek.” 1970.**  
   Primary text on the project, useful for understanding how SEEK was framed as a responsive machine environment.  
   [PDF source](https://worrydream.com/refs/Negroponte_1970_-_Seek.pdf)

2. **Negroponte, Nicholas. “Terms and Conditions.” _e-flux Architecture_, Digital X, 2019.**  
   Later reflection on participation, computation, flexibility, and the culture of technological demonstration around the Architecture Machine Group.  
   [Article source](https://www.e-flux.com/architecture/digital-x/260419/terms-and-conditions)

### Critique & Comments from Others

1. **Shepard, Mark. “ARC 605: (re)seeking architecture.” Center for Architecture and Situated Technologies, University at Buffalo, October 9, 2018.**  
   A strong architectural reading of SEEK as an early attempt to integrate artificial intelligence and architecture, while also naming its technical and ethical failures.  
   [Article source](https://ubwp.buffalo.edu/cast/2018/10/09/arc-605-reseeking-architecture/)

2. **Cybernetic Zoo. “1969-70 - SEEK - Nicholas Negroponte (American).” _Cybernetic Zoo_, October 27, 2010; updated December 30, 2011.**  
   Useful for visual and technical documentation of the Plexiglas enclosure, blocks, gerbils, robotic arm, and historical descriptions of the installation.  
   [Article source](https://cyberneticzoo.com/robots-in-art/1969-70-seek-nicholas-negroponte-american/)

3. **Emerson, Lori. “Selling the Future at the MIT Media Lab.” February 17, 2016.**  
   Contextual critique of the institutional culture and futurist rhetoric surrounding the MIT Media Lab and its technological imagination.  
   [Article source](https://loriemerson.net/2016/02/17/selling-the-future-at-the-mit-media-lab/)
